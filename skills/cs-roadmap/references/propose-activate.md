# Propose outcome tasks and activate work

## Form independently verifiable tasks

Each item needs a stable id/title, kind, observable outcome and acceptance, risk level, side-effect boundary, real dependencies/contracts and required validation access. Add rollout/rollback only where the risk requires it.

Prefer vertical outcomes over activity buckets such as backend, frontend or database. Avoid fake ordering, giant items and ceremony-only fragments. States describe coordination (`queued`, `ready`, `active`, `completed`, `dropped`); they are not a substitute for each task's Harness completion.

## Challenge the decomposition

Look for missing outcomes, undefined contracts, duplicated scope, false dependencies, blocked authority, unobservable acceptance and high-risk work without evidence access. Revise the roadmap as facts change.

## Activate without a hand-off

When execution was requested and an item is ready, create its task contract, link only the necessary roadmap/contracts and continue into the matching outcome lens in the same invocation. Do not stop at a document or instruct the user to invoke another skill.

Keep a roadmap artifact only when humans or Agents will use it to coordinate future work, and maintain its index. Child work becomes completed only through its own evidence gate.
